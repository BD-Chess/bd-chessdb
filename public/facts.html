Detailed Explanations

Champions vs. ChessDB

In the past, learning from a grandmaster or world champion was the pinnacle of chess education. Today, ChessDB’s billion-node, backward-propagated chess engine database delivers superior move quality instantly — outclassing any human panel or a top chess engine (e.g. Stockfish) run on a supercomputer.

Precomputed CPU‑Years

Each position’s depth‑50 search takes ~200 s on a typical CPU (~20 M nodes at 100 k nodes/s). Times ~48 billion positions → ~9.6×10¹² s ≈ 304 000 CPU‑years—and that work is already done.

Move Time Savings

A 4-core PC needs 30–60 minutes to reach depth 50. ChessDB returns the same analysis in under a second, saving you up to an hour every move.

Backward Propagation

Deep searches of child positions propagate upward, enriching parent evaluations beyond flat search depth.

  • Greater depth: Children can reach 60+ plies, boosting parent accuracy.
  • Stability: Multiple child lines reduce score variance.
  • Accuracy: Ranks moves more precisely than a single-pass engine.

Live AI Fallback

When a position isn’t yet in the database, ChessDB seamlessly uses a live Stockfish instance so you always have guidance.

API & UCI Extensions

Use HTTP endpoints (queryall, querybest, querypv, etc.) or UCI commands (bookmove, hint, egtbmove) to integrate with your own GUI or engine.

Engine‑Built Opening Book

Exhaustive engine analysis, not human game pruning, generates a complete, bias-free opening repertoire.

High-Quality Line Curation

As of early 2025, the ChessDB team has curated over 955,000 opening lines that passed strict internal quality checks known as the Grob Test Suite. These lines meet criteria such as deep analysis, variation completeness, and tactical reliability.

While these lines aren’t yet published to the public engine book, they represent a major expansion of coverage — particularly for rare or underexplored openings — and will be integrated in a future update.
